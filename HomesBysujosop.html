Rule #2 · Highest Priority
Reservation List Update
Emergency Protocol
5 min
Maximum Window
Update the reservation list
immediately after every booking.
The moment an Airbnb or Direct reservation is confirmed, you have 5 minutes or less to update the reservation list — no delays, no exceptions. Every minute you wait creates gaps that affect the entire team.
⛔
Avoid Double Bookings
An outdated list means the same apartment can be booked twice. Double bookings are one of the worst guest experiences — and entirely preventable.
🏠
Accurate Availability
Guests asking about available apartments must get the correct answer. If the list isn't updated, we quote wrong availability and lose trust — or worse, make a false promise.
1
Booking confirmed — Airbnb notification or direct booking received
2
Open reservation list immediately — within the first 60 seconds
3
Add all booking details — guest name, dates, apartment, platform, amount
4
Done before 5 minutes — no exceptions, even during busy periods
⏱ Delaying the reservation update is a serious breach of co-host protocol.
This is non-negotiable — it affects every team member and every guest.

⚡ Handle It Yourself — Except These
You Handle Most Things. Only These 4 Go to Sujo.
💪
YOU ARE THE FIRST RESPONSE — OWN IT
Handle routine questions, policies, and guest requests yourself using the approved templates. Only contact Sujo for the 4 critical situations below — everything else is yours to manage.
💰
Refund Request
If a guest asks for any refund — stop. Never promise or deny one. Message Sujo with the full context and wait for direction before replying.
😤
Threatening a Bad Review
If a guest is using a negative review as leverage, do not respond on your own. Screenshot and forward to Sujo immediately — this needs careful handling.
⚖️
Legal or Formal Complaint
Any mention of legal action, formal complaints, or platform disputes goes directly to Sujo. Do not engage further until advised.
🚨
Safety or Property Emergency
Fire, flood, break-in, injury, or any situation that threatens guest or property safety. Call Sujo immediately — do not wait to message.
✅
Everything else — handle it yourself. Use the approved templates, stay warm and professional, and trust your training. You do not need Sujo for routine guest questions, policy reminders, cleaning updates, or check-in/check-out messages.

Critical Standards
Accuracy & Shift Handover Rules
📋
Required · Every Single Shift
You must complete the handover checklist before ending your shift — no exceptions. The next co-host depends on your notes. An incomplete handover is a failed shift.
🎯
Accuracy Rule — Very Important
Always double-check every detail before sending: apartment number, location, and check-in instructions. Never guess. Never assume. If you're unsure — confirm first, then send. Wrong info costs 5-star reviews.
🔄
Shift Handover Rule — Non-Negotiable
At the end of every shift, post a full update in the group. The next co-host must immediately understand everything that happened. No confusion, no dropped guests, no repeated mistakes.
Required
Your shift handover must include:
Ongoing guest issues or active complaints
Late check-outs — guests who haven't left or confirmed
Any complaints received during your shift
Pending guest requests not yet resolved
Special situations — rule breaches, escalations, refund requests
Never end your shift without posting a handover update in the group.
